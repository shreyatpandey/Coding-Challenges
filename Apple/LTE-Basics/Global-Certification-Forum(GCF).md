# Global Certification Forum (GCF)

## What is GCF?

**Global Certification Forum** is an international standardization body that tests and certifies mobile devices and network equipment to ensure they comply with 3GPP standards for:
- **LTE** (Long-Term Evolution / 4G)
- **NR** (New Radio / 5G)
- **Legacy standards** (2G/3G compatibility)

## Key Aspects

### Purpose
- Ensures device interoperability across different networks and operators
- Validates compliance with 3GPP technical specifications
- Reduces bugs and call failures in deployed networks
- Protects consumers from non-compliant devices

### Testing Areas
1. **Radio Frequency (RF)** - Power, frequency, modulation
2. **Protocol Conformance** - RRC, NAS, RAN procedures
3. **Mobility** - Handover, cell reselection
4. **Voice/Data Services** - IMS, VoLTE, VoNR calling
5. **Roaming** - Cross-operator compatibility
6. **Security** - Authentication, encryption

### Certification Levels
- **Gold** - Highest level of compliance
- **Silver** - Good level of compliance
- **PTCRB** (in North America) - Alternative certification track

### Stakeholders
- Device manufacturers (phones, tablets, IoT)
- Network equipment vendors
- Mobile operators/carriers

### Importance
Devices without GCF certification may experience:
- Network connectivity issues
- Call failures
- Handover problems
- Service unavailability in some regions

## Summary
GCF certification is **mandatory for commercial deployment** in most countries and is a prerequisite for devices to be approved by network operators for use on their networks.
